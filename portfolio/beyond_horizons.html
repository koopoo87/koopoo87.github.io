Beyond Horizons : A Conceptual Digital Clock





Concept

Beyond Horizons is an conceptual disk-shaped clock that was aimed to give users a moment of meditative experience.

The thin disk displays the current time with small metal balls anchored with the magnetic force from array of magnets attached to the bottom layer of the disk.

While slowly spinning on the surface, the metal balls shows beautiful kinetic movements while repeatedly dispersing away or flocking together with beautiful sound.

The current prototype is made as a conceptual clock design leaving implementation of the automatic number display as a future work.


Designing Process : Mechanisms

The biggest challenge of Beyond Horizon was to make a thin, sleek disk spinning beautifully with the simplest mechanism with the closest distance from the surface.

Several trials proved that tilting approximately 5 degrees from the surface showed the most aesthetic movment.

Following sketch illustrates several possible types of mechanisms and its required components. Both the 3D printer and the laser cutter were utilised throughout the design process.





After numbers of trial and error, following mechanism was fixed as the final design.





I made the disk sustained only from the center point with the axis of the motor. It gave the disks the full degree of freedom as in the principles from the ball joint.

To prevent the disk(display) from spinning together with the motor axis, I connected one part of the disk with the rubber band that loosely anchors the disk part but sustaining mininum amount of force to move disk up and down.

As an interactive user input, Leap motion sensor was attached to the device to recognize user's hand gestures.

The clockwise gesture triggers the clock to move in clock-wise diretion and vice versa. Processing was used to connect leap motion sensor and Arduino.

3D Modeling

Inventor 2015 was used to simulate mechanisms. The spinning part attached to the stepper motor is exported to print with 3D printers.
